Students For Free and Open Internet

Open Access means unrestricted access to peer-reviewed scholarly research via Internet. Public access to journals had been started since 1970’s when computer scientists had started self-archiving their research journals in anonymous File Transfer Protocol(FTP) archives, while physicists had been self-archiving in arxiv since 1990’s. In 1994, Subversive Proposal has been started to generalise the practise.

Vision

The Freedom Online Coalition is an aggregation of governments focused on working together for developing Internet Freedom. As Internet suppression expands far and wide, the Coalition furnishes a discussion for similarly invested governments to work with civil social order and the private partners in a multi-stakeholder methodology to backing the capability of people to practice their human rights and essential opportunities on the web. In December two thousand eleven, the first Freedom Online Conference was held at The Hague, Netherlands. The Conference discussed, evangelized and came up with the outcome declaration for the makeup of a Coalition Membership Movement for Internet Freedom. The basic agenda of the coalition is facilitation to protect and spread the word for Internet Freedom and to pro-actively further the freedom on Internet. This coalition also brings together the countries to a common platform which is towards a common worldwide goal, keep the internet Free and Open.
